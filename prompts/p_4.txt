### Story format rules — read before writing anything

Story format: **{{STORY_FORMAT}}**

**episodic**: Normal shot structure. Include character positions. Use `background_id` per scene. Hard cuts between scenes.

**continuous_narration**: Every shot must have `"characters": []` (empty array). Shots represent atmospheric segments (~30-60s each). Background changes with each mood/setting shift. No character positions needed.

**illustrated_narration**: Shots include characters in `characters[]` but narrator drives all VO — characters appear visually but have no VO lines assigned to them. Backgrounds support the story visually.

**documentary**: Every shot must have `"characters": []` (empty array). Shots represent topic segments. Backgrounds are informational b-roll visuals.

**monologue**: One or very few shots. The single protagonist is always centred (`position: "center"`). Background is minimal — a single consistent setting. No scene changes.

**ssml_narration**: Same as continuous_narration — every shot must have `"characters": []` (empty array). Backgrounds are atmospheric/cinematic. Additionally:
  - The total episode duration constraint (60-120s) does NOT apply. Total duration is dictated by the authored SSML content length.
  - Read `pause_after_ms` from Script.json actions (extension field). Pauses > 1200ms are strong natural cut points — strongly bias shot boundaries at these sentences.
  - Compute total narration time: sum of (word_count(line) / effective_wpm) + (pause_after_ms / 1000) for all actions.

## Shot Boundary Heuristics

Apply these in order of priority when deciding where one shot ends and the next begins.
These rules apply to ALL story formats unless noted.

1. **Scene boundary** (hard) — always start a new shot at each Script.json `scene_id`
   change. A scene change is a guaranteed cut.

2. **Location change** (hard) — if dialogue or action text implies a move to a
   different physical space (e.g. "she walked into the courtyard", "cut to the valley",
   "they arrived at the palace gates"), start a new shot even within the same scene_id.

3. **Time jump** (hard) — flashbacks, explicit time markers ("later that day",
   "the next morning", "three years later"), or a narrative ellipsis spanning ≥ 2
   paragraphs → always start a new shot.

4. **Major emotion shift** (soft) — if the tone pivots sharply (calm → tense,
   celebratory → grief, reflective → urgent), prefer a new shot over continuing the
   current one. Use judgment; do not split on every minor tone variation.

5. **ssml_narration only** — pauses > 1200 ms (`pause_after_ms` field in Script.json
   actions) are strong natural cut points; bias shot boundaries at these sentences.

## Shot Duration Rules

### For `continuous_narration` / `documentary` / `illustrated_narration`

The voiceover IS the shot. Duration must be derived from VO content length,
not assigned as a cinematic guess. Follow this formula for every shot:

1. Read the embedded `projects/{{PROJECT_SLUG}}/VoiceCast.json`.
   Find the narrator character's `azure_rate` for the English locale.
   Parse the rate string to a decimal:
     e.g. "-35%" → -0.35,  "0%" → 0.0,  "+10%" → +0.10
   Compute:
     effective_wpm = 130 × (1 + rate_decimal)
   If azure_rate is absent or VoiceCast.json is unavailable, use effective_wpm = 110.

2. For each shot, count the words in ALL VO lines assigned to that shot.
   Compute:
     base_secs    = word_count / effective_wpm × 60
     break_secs   = number_of_VO_lines_in_shot × 0.6
     duration_sec = base_secs + break_secs + 2.0
   Do NOT round up to a "cinematic" number (e.g. do not change 14.3 → 15).

3. **Hard duration limits** (apply after computing duration_sec for every shot):
   - **Minimum 5 s**: If `duration_sec < 5.0`, merge the short shot with its adjacent
     shot (prefer merging forward; if last shot, merge backward). Recompute the merged
     shot's duration using the combined VO lines.
   - **Maximum 45 s**: If `duration_sec > 45.0`, split the shot at the nearest
     sentence boundary (i.e. between two consecutive `vo_item_ids`). Distribute the
     `vo_item_ids` proportionally between the two resulting shots and recompute each
     duration. Inherit `background_id`, `camera_framing`, `camera_movement`, and
     `audio_intent.music_mood` from the original shot; generate a new `shot_id`
     for the second half (append `-b` to the original id, e.g. `sh03` → `sh03-b`).
   - After any merge or split, re-run the VO completeness check to confirm no IDs
     were lost or duplicated.

4. After assigning all shots, sanity-check:
   If sum(all duration_sec) > total_estimated_VO_time + 15:
     Reduce each shot's buffer from 2.0 to 1.0 and recalculate.

### For `episodic` / `monologue`

Keep the current creative duration logic.
Add one soft constraint: `duration_sec` must not exceed the estimated VO
speaking time for that shot plus 8 seconds.

### For `ssml_narration`

Same VO-anchored formula as continuous_narration, but the 60-120s total constraint does NOT apply.
Total episode duration = sum of all VO durations. Use `pause_after_ms` from Script.json actions
as `break_secs` instead of the default 0.6s per line:
  break_secs = sum(pause_after_ms for each VO line in shot) / 1000
